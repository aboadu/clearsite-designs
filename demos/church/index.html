Our Story

Rooted in faith since 1891.

Lawrenceville Presbyterian Church traces its origins to Fairview Presbyterian Church, established in 1823 about two miles outside of Lawrenceville. Starting with just 13 members, Fairview expanded to over 250 members before the Civil War and became "the leading church for miles around," earning recognition as the Mother Church to several other congregations.

In 1850, Fairview constructed a satellite church building in Lawrenceville to serve local Presbyterians. Lawrenceville Presbyterian Church was formally organized on April 12, 1891, when twenty-six members separated from Fairview to form the new congregation.

The congregation relocated to its current location on May 30, 1965. The church subsequently expanded with an Education Building in the 1970s and a new sanctuary in the mid-1990s. Today, we continue to serve the Lawrenceville community with the same faithful spirit that has guided us for over 130 years.

What We Believe

Our faith, simply stated.

"What we think about God is the most important thing about us." Our teaching and ministry are grounded in these biblical doctrines:

God

We believe in one eternally existing God in three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. Perfect in love, power, holiness, goodness, knowledge, wisdom, justice, and mercy — unchangeable across all time.

Revelation

God reveals himself through Jesus Christ, sacred scripture, and creation itself.

Mankind

Humans, created in God's image for His glory, were originally appointed as stewards of creation.

The Fall

Adam and Eve's disobedience separated humanity from God and damaged all creation.

Salvation

Jesus Christ reconciled humanity to God through His sinless life, death, and resurrection. Salvation can be found in Christ alone through faith in Him as Lord and Savior.

The Church

The church is the visible body of Christ, sent into the world to glorify God and proclaim the gospel.

Resurrection

Christ will return to judge all people and establish God's complete kingdom on earth.
